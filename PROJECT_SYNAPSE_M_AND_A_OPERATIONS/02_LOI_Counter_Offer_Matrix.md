## Post-Teardown Negotiation Playbook

*After your T+72h hard stop scripts fire and the network ports close, use these three rules to control the narrative when the buyer's Corp Dev team reaches out.*

### Rule 1: Lead with the Audit Logs

Pull `data/finalized_audits/audit_log_<buyer>_closed.csv`. It contains every 60-second snapshot of their team's connection.

**The script:** "Our cloud telemetry logs confirm your technical team ran extensive multi-user burst loops across [N] active nodes, maintaining peak processing performance while remaining well within our hard container allocation limits of 2 CPU cores and 512MB RAM. The data speaks for itself — you have already verified the three payout metrics independently."

### Rule 2: Block Product-Linked Earnouts

If they try to introduce complex, product-adoption-based earnouts to reduce upfront risk, point to the three deterministic engineering benchmarks already confirmed during their session:

1. **Throughput:** Confirmed at 11,000+ samples/sec (60× real-time at 250 Hz)
2. **Latency:** Confirmed under 250 µs total pipeline latency (measured at 206 µs)
3. **Security:** Confirmed 100% cryptographic payload isolation via AES-256-GCM

**The strategy:** Enforce that any milestone-based compensation is strictly tied to these three code-level verifications run within 10 days of closing — completely independent of the buyer's future commercial rollout timeline or product management choices. No revenue hooks, no user adoption targets.

### Rule 3: Anchor on the IP Monopoly

If the buyer hesitates during the 14-day exclusivity window, remind them of the defensive value established by the Provisional Patent Claims Schedule (Class 1 + Class 2 claims covering unsupervised covariance adaptation and context-aware temporal neural correction).

**The script:** "Project Synapse is not an optimization script — it is a lock-out asset. The conglomerate that acquires it immediately gains an exclusive, calibration-free user interface layer that blocks their direct market competitors from matching their product's fluidity, power metrics, and responsiveness. Every quarter your competitor goes without this capability is a quarter they cannot close."
